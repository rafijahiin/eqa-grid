Detailed Comparison of Key Criteria

Criterion Old Guideline (May 2024 Version) New Guideline (Annotated 2025 Version)
Sampling Strategy (Q8.iii) The grid asked if a sampling strategy was provided and described how diverse perspectives were captured. The guidance was not prescriptive. This is a new, mandatory requirement. The report must detail the selection rationale, criteria, sample size, and measures taken to ensure representativeness and minimize bias.
Actionable Recommendations (Q16.ii) Stated that recommendations should be "useful and actionable" with "specific guidance" for implementation, such as actions, deadlines, and responsible actors. The requirement is much stricter. Recommendations must now be categorized (strategic/programmatic), include suggested actions, and precisely identify the responsible unit (e.g., "M&E Unit," not just "Country Office").
Recommendation Priority (Q16.iv) Required recommendations to be "prioritized based on their importance, urgency, and potential impact." A specific system is now mandated. A three-tiered prioritization system (low, medium, high) must be applied to all recommendations.
UN SWAP: GEEW Integration (Q21.i) A "Fully integrated" rating was available, with guidance provided in a separate box explaining the conditions. The threshold is higher and more explicit. To be rated "Fully integrated," the evaluation must meet four specific, listed conditions related to scope, questions, criteria, and analysis.
Adequacy of Findings (Q11.i) The grid asked if findings were presented clearly and provided "sufficient levels of evidence" to address evaluation questions. A quantitative threshold has been introduced. The criterion is now met if more than 75% of the findings are clearly presented and substantiated by sufficient evidence.
Linkages to SDGs (New: Q5.iii) This was part of the general background description (Q2.iii), stating "Linkages drawn between the evaluand and the ICPD benchmarks and SDGs". This is now a distinct criterion. The background section must provide an overview of the status of ICPD-related SDGs, with a focus on SDGs 3, 5, and 10.

Detailed Breakdown of New Requirements

Sampling Strategy

What's New: This is a brand new requirement. You must now provide a detailed description of your sampling strategy, including the rationale, criteria, size, and steps taken to ensure it's representative and unbiased.

Why it Matters: This ensures that the evaluation captures diverse perspectives, especially from vulnerable groups, justifying that the right people were included.

What to do: Add a dedicated subsection in your methodology that covers your sampling rationale, criteria, size, and bias mitigation measures.

Actionable Recommendations

What's New: Another new requirement. Recommendations must now specify the operational implications and precisely identify the responsible units (e.g., M&E Unit, not just the Country Office).

Why it Matters: This prevents recommendations from being too vague. Naming a specific unit creates clear ownership and accountability.

What to do: For every recommendation, update it to name the specific functional unit responsible for implementation.

Recommendation Priority

What's New: All recommendations must now be prioritized using a three-tiered system (low, medium, high).

Why it Matters: Prioritization helps stakeholders focus on the most urgent and impactful actions first.

What to do: Ensure every single recommendation is tagged with a "High," "Medium," or "Low" priority level.

Analysis Methods & AI Use

What's New: You must now clearly describe your analysis methods, and if you used Artificial Intelligence (AI), you must fully disclose and explain how it was used.

Why it Matters: This increases transparency and trust in the evaluation's findings, especially when using new technologies.

What to do: If AI was used (e.g., for data analysis), add a clear description in the methodology section explaining what you used it for and that a human verified the results.

Linkages to SDGs

What's New: The background section of the report must now explain how the program being evaluated connects to relevant Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), especially SDGs 3, 5, and 10.

Why it Matters: This grounds the evaluation in a broader global context and demonstrates its relevance to international development priorities.

What to do: In your background/context section, make sure to reference data and indicators related to SDGs 3 (Good Health), 5 (Gender Equality), and 10 (Reduced Inequalities).
